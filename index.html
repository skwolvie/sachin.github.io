    ‚≠êEffect of Traffic Congestion, & Firm‚Äôs Work from Home Policies on Employee‚Äôs Work-Life-Balance
    Research Areas: Natural Language Processing, Job Markets, Econometrics, Data Science
    Advisors: Deepa Mani,Sumeet Kumar
    Stage: Exploratory Data Analysis
    Summary:

    This study aims at analyzing the impact of city‚Äôs traffic on the employee's Work-Life Balance. We utilized CEIC's top six Indian Cities Traffic Congestion Dataset and mapped a prior 30-day average of the traffic index for each date of review. We used occupation, industry, firm, and City level controls in our regression analysis and observed that the traffic index has a significant negative impact on Work-Life Balance. Further, we observe that during lockdown the traffic index had a significant positive impact on work-life balance provided that the employee had the benefits of work from home.


    ‚≠êDifferential Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on the male & female dominant occupations
    Research Areas:Job Markets, Diversity & Inclusion in Workplace, Econometrics
    Advisors: Deepa Mani, Nikhil Madan
    Stage: Exploratory Data Analysis
    Summary:

    We examined the differential impact of the COVID-19 lockdown on the Male and Female Dominant Occupations. We Mapped the NCO 2015 Codes to the Job titles reported by the employees in the Ambition Box Employee Reviews Dataset by performing a string-similarity mapping of Job Titles to ONET Job Titles and SOC Codes, and then a crosswalk from SOC to NCO through ISCO coding system. We Mapped the NCO codes to the PLFS 2019-2020 Survey‚Äôs percentage of female proportion in an NCO Occupation. We built interactive visual graphs & descriptive statistics using the engineered dataset. We conducted Regression Analysis and discovered the significant decline in the work-life balance and overall job satisfaction of employees in the women-dominated occupations compared to the male-dominated occupations.
